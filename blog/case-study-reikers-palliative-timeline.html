QoL Metric: "Desire to bite" (Play drive)

Unvarnished timeline of medication, decline, death. Not prescription guide - record of how advocacy bought high-quality years.

Phase 0: Baseline Years (2018-2022)

Managed for orthopedic maintenance + anxiety. Key: healthy baseline bloodwork = aggressive med options later.

  • April 2021 (Lump removal) - Post-op: Trazodone for activity restriction. Proof: sedation = kindness, not punishment
  • July 2022 (Broken canine) - Pre-dental: 300mg Gabapentin BID + Meloxicam. Geriatric panel "pretty great" = liver/kidneys can handle long-term NSAIDs

Phase 1: Senior Years (2023-2024)

Shift to daily management. Trial, assess, pivot.

March 2023: Librela Trial

Monthly injections for OA.

  • Result (July): "Didn't see effects"
  • Lesson: Failed trial = critical data. Pain not NGF-driven (inflammatory) → suggests neuropathic/central. Stop + pivot

July 2023: Poly-Pharmacy Begins

  • Gabapentin 300mg daily
  • Acetaminophen BID - non-inflammatory pain
  • Cartrophen weekly loading

Phase 2: Aggressive Escalation (Early 2025)

February 2025: Decline in stability (ataxia/knuckling). No "wait and see." Email rapid-fire protocol adjustment.

1. Amantadine Pivot (March 2025)

  • March 13 email: Request Amantadine (NMDA antagonist) for wind-up pain
  • Reaction: Vomiting, acute liquid diarrhea
  • Pivot: Dr. Reid email - pause, treat symptoms, restart lower dose later
  • Key: Adverse event ≠ stop. Pause + recalibrate

2. Ketamine (Spring 2025)

  • Protocol: 0.5mg/kg SQ
  • May 21 email: Monthly shots wearing off after 3 weeks. "Try at 14 day mark?"
  • Vet: "No problem q 2 weeks"
  • October: Weekly injections to maintain mobility

Key Advocacy Moment

Email-based adjustment = effective communication. Observed duration, requested frequency change based on functional decline = real-time therapy optimization.

Phase 3: Rollercoaster (Spring-Fall 2025)

  • April 11 crisis: Low point (GI + ataxia). Euthanasia discussed
  • April 12 rally: "Doing ok!" Near-death dip → rally = classic palliative trajectory. Meds on board (Acetaminophen, Ketamine, Gabapentin) = bounce back for 8 more quality months

The Data (Oct 28, 2025 - Jan 2, 2026)

28 tracked days: 22 good, 6 neutral, 0 bad. Objective evidence QoL acceptable this period.

Phase 4: Terminal Phase (January 2026)

Neurodegeneration overwhelmed pharmacology. "Desire to Bite" metric collapsed.

Phase 4: Terminal Phase (January 2026)

By January, the neurodegeneration overwhelmed the pharmacological support. The "Desire to Bite" metric—his primary joy—collapsed.

January 7, 2026 (The "Hail Mary" Email)

  • Status: "Lost almost all hind leg function... drag pads-up"
  • Proposal: "Request single dose methadone home trial... If pain shows, at least I'll know"
  • Vet: Agreed. SQ Methadone 0.23ml q8h
  • Result: Profound sedation + comfort, but functional deficits permanent. Pain managed, body gone

January 10: Metric Failure

  • 2/5 (Agree) - "Does not want to play"
  • 1/5 (Strongly Agree) - "Does not enjoy same activities"

Primary QoL metric (play drive) consistently failed = decision clear.

January 13, 2026

Euthanized at Oriole Road.

What Bought Time

Nearly 1 year after first major "wobble" (Feb 2025). Bought with:

  1. Acetaminophen - Cheap, effective, started early
  2. Ketamine - Heavy hitter for central sensitization, owner-adjusted frequency
  3. Communication - Email adjustments (Amantadine, Methadone) = immediate diagnostics
  4. Tracking - Good/bad day calendars = data, not emotion
  5. Clear metrics - "Desire to bite" = objective endpoint

Clinical Lessons

1. Failed Trials = Data

Librela "failed" but gave critical info about pain nature. Unsuccessful trials narrow diagnostic field.

2. Email Protocol Adjustments Work

Ketamine frequency + amantadine troubleshooting via email. Reduced visits/stress, real-time optimization based on owner observation.

3. Diagnostic Trial

Methadone at end = diagnostic tool differentiating pain from neuro decline. Function didn't improve despite analgesia = answer obtained.

4. Define QoL in Advance

Reiker: "Desire to bite." Others: greeting family, eating, sleeping comfortably. Define early, track objectively. See QoL Assessments for tools.

5. Aggressive ≠ Inappropriate

Weekly ketamine, daily acetaminophen, multiple NMDA antagonist trials. Not reckless = systematic, monitored, function-based. Aggressive palliative care (properly managed) extends quality time, not just survival.

Not a Prescription

Every case different. Reiker's protocol for his presentation: neurodegenerative with preserved mentation, declining motor. Your dog's situation/bloodwork/comorbidities/QoL metrics will differ. Use as advocacy/communication framework, not medication checklist.

How to Use This Case

  1. Establish baseline bloodwork - Healthy organs = more options later
  2. Track good vs. bad days - Calendar/app (HosPet) for objective data
  3. Define metric - What activity/behavior non-negotiable for QoL?
  4. Communicate frequently - Email observations. Good vets appreciate data-driven owners
  5. Trial + pivot - Med doesn't work? Stop, move on. Failed trials = useful info
  6. Know when to stop - Primary metric fails consistently = honor dog by letting go. See End-of-Life Planning.

Aggressive advocacy, systematic medication management, peaceful end when function irretrievable. Not perfect, but data-driven = clearer hard decisions.
