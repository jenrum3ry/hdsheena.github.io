Patient: Reiker (Belgian Malinois, 15yo)

Diagnoses: Neurodegenerative myelopathy (suspected DM), Osteoarthritis, Sarcopenia

Care Team: Dr. Andrew Reid & Dr. Fiona Reid (Oriole Road), Dr. Allen Jacobson (Sitara)

QoL Metric: "Desire to bite" (Play drive)

Unvarnished timeline of medication, decline, death. Not prescription guide - record of how advocacy bought high-quality years.

Phase 0: Baseline Years (2018-2022)

Managed for orthopedic maintenance + anxiety. Key: healthy baseline bloodwork = aggressive med options later.

  • April 2021 (Lump removal) - Post-op: Trazodone for activity restriction. Proof: sedation = kindness, not punishment
  • July 2022 (Broken canine) - Pre-dental: 300mg Gabapentin BID + Meloxicam. Geriatric panel "pretty great" = liver/kidneys can handle long-term NSAIDs

Phase 1: Senior Years (2023-2024)

Shift to daily management. Trial, assess, pivot.

March 2023: Librela Trial

Monthly injections for OA.

  • Result (July): "Didn't see effects"
  • Lesson: Failed trial = critical data. Pain not NGF-driven (inflammatory) → suggests neuropathic/central. Stop + pivot

July 2023: Poly-Pharmacy Begins

  • Gabapentin 300mg daily
  • Acetaminophen BID - non-inflammatory pain
  • Cartrophen weekly loading

Phase 2: Aggressive Escalation (Early 2025)

February 2025: Decline in stability (ataxia/knuckling). No "wait and see." Email rapid-fire protocol adjustment.

1. Amantadine Pivot (March 2025)

  • March 13 email: Request Amantadine (NMDA antagonist) for wind-up pain
  • Reaction: Vomiting, acute liquid diarrhea
  • Pivot: Dr. Reid email - pause, treat symptoms, restart lower dose later
  • Key: Adverse event ≠ stop. Pause + recalibrate

2. Ketamine (Spring 2025)

  • Protocol: 0.5mg/kg SQ
  • May 21 email: Monthly shots wearing off after 3 weeks. "Try at 14 day mark?"
  • Vet: "No problem q 2 weeks"
  • October: Weekly injections to maintain mobility

Key Advocacy Moment

Email-based adjustment = effective communication. Observed duration, requested frequency change based on functional decline = real-time therapy optimization.

Phase 3: Rollercoaster (Spring-Fall 2025)

  • April 11 crisis: Low point (GI + ataxia). Euthanasia discussed
  • April 12 rally: "Doing ok!" Near-death dip → rally = classic palliative trajectory. Meds on board (Acetaminophen, Ketamine, Gabapentin) = bounce back for 8 more quality months

The Data (Oct 28, 2025 - Jan 2, 2026)

28 tracked days: 22 good, 6 neutral, 0 bad. Objective evidence QoL acceptable this period.

Phase 4: Terminal Phase (January 2026)

By January, the neurodegeneration overwhelmed the pharmacological support. The "Desire to Bite" metric—his primary joy—collapsed.

January 7, 2026 (The "Hail Mary" Email)

  • Status: You wrote, "He's lost almost all function in his hind legs... drag behind him pads-up."
  • The Proposal: "I'd like to request a single dose... of methadone to try at home... If he shows me that much of what I'm seeing is pain... at least I'll know."
  • The Vet Response: Dr. Reid agreed to a home trial of subcutaneous Methadone (0.23ml q8h).
  • The Result: The Methadone provided profound sedation and comfort, but it confirmed the functional deficits were permanent. The "pain" was managed, but the "body" was gone.

January 10, 2026 (Metric Failure)

  • Scored 2/5 (Agree) on "Does not want to play"
  • Scored 1/5 (Strongly Agree) on "Does not enjoy same activities"

When the primary quality-of-life metric—play drive—consistently failed, the decision became clear.

The End (January 13, 2026)

Reiker was euthanized on Tuesday, January 13, 2026, at Oriole Road Animal Hospital.

Summary of What Bought Us Time

Reiker lived nearly a full year after his first major "wobble" in February 2025. That year was bought with:

  1. Acetaminophen: Cheap, effective, and started early
  2. Ketamine: The "heavy hitter" for central sensitization, adjusted by owner observation
  3. Communication: The "Amantadine failure" and "Methadone Trial" were not mistakes; they were diagnostics run via email to get immediate answers
  4. Objective Tracking: Good day/bad day calendars provided data, not just emotion
  5. Clear Metrics: "Desire to bite" as the primary quality-of-life indicator gave us an objective endpoint

Clinical Lessons from Reiker's Case

1. Failed Trials Are Data

Librela "failed" but gave critical pain type information. Unsuccessful trials narrow diagnostic field, not setbacks.

2. Email Protocol Adjustments Work

Ketamine frequency + amantadine troubleshooting via email = reduced clinic visits/stress, real-time optimization from owner observation.

3. "Diagnostic Trial" of Methadone

Used at end as diagnostic to differentiate pain from neurological decline. Function didn't improve despite profound analgesia = answer clear.

4. Define QoL in Advance

For Reiker: "Desire to bite." For others: greeting family, eating enthusiastically, sleeping comfortably. Define early, track objectively.

5. Aggressive ≠ Inappropriate

Ketamine weekly, acetaminophen daily, trialed multiple NMDA antagonists. Systematic, monitored, function-based. Aggressive palliative care when properly managed extends quality time, not just survival.

Not a Prescription

Every case different. Reiker's protocol worked for his presentation: neurodegenerative disease with preserved mentation, declining motor function. Your dog's situation will differ. Use as framework for advocacy/communication, not medication checklist.

How to Use This Case Study

  1. Establish Baseline Bloodwork: Healthy kidneys/liver = more pharmacological options later
  2. Track Good vs. Bad Days: Calendar or app creates objective data
  3. Define Your Metric: What activity/behavior is non-negotiable for your dog's QoL?
  4. Communicate Frequently: Email vet with observations. Good vets appreciate data-driven owners
  5. Trial and Pivot: Medication doesn't work? Stop, move on. Failed trials = useful info
  6. Know When to Stop: When primary metric fails consistently, honor your dog by letting go

Reiker's story: aggressive advocacy, systematic medication management, peaceful end when function couldn't be restored. Not perfect, but data-driven—making hard decisions clearer.

In Memory of Reiker (2011–2026)

A good boy who taught us everything about advocacy, data-driven care, and knowing when to let go.
