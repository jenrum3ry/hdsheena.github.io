Being a veterinary professional with a senior dog = dual role: trained expert + emotional pet parent. Use knowledge for advocacy while managing professional challenges.

Knowledge: Advantages & Challenges

Advantages: Early recognition, informed questions, technical understanding, at-home care skills, professional network, realistic expectations.

Challenges: No emotional distance, knowledge of worst-case scenarios, early anticipatory grief, blurred professional-personal boundaries.

Compassion fatigue support: NAVTA Wellness

  • Emotional Distance Impossible: Can't compartmentalize like you do at work
  • Knowledge of Worst-Case Scenarios: You've seen how bad things can get
  • Anticipatory Grief: Starts earlier because you know what's coming
  • Professional-Personal Boundary Blur: Difficulty separating your roles

Note: If you're struggling with compassion fatigue, see the CVMA Wellness Resources or your provincial veterinary technician association (OAVT, RVTTBC, etc.) for professional support.

Effective Advocacy During Appointments

Setting the Right Tone

You don't need scripts—you know how to talk to a vet. You just need permission to be assertive.

The approach: "I'm an RVT/VT, so I'm familiar with medical terminology. Can we skip the client-speak and discuss this peer-to-peer?"

Effective Advocacy During Appointments

Set the tone: "I'm an RVT. Can we discuss peer-to-peer?"

Use technical language:

  • "Concerned about central sensitization. NMDA antagonist protocol?"
  • "Tracking trendlines, not just today's status. Here's data."
  • "Pain interference scores suggest revisiting analgesia ladder."
  • "Maropitant for visceral component?"

If vet isn't receptive = need different partner.

When to Speak Up

  • Noticed something missed in exam
  • Plan doesn't align with AAHA/IAAHPC Guidelines
  • Drug interaction concerns
  • Aware of specialists/resources

If dismissive: quote guidelines, present data, request referral.

Professional Network

  • Second opinions from colleagues/specialists
  • Referral recommendations
  • VIN access - pull literature yourself
  • Peer support

Technical Skills: Your Advantage

Weekly Diagnostic Monitoring

  • Sleeping Respiratory Rate (SRR) - Catch CHF weeks early. Normal: <30 breaths/min sleeping
  • Bladder Palpation - Check retention. Distended bladder = emergency
  • Cardiac Auscultation - New murmurs, arrhythmias
  • BCS & MCS - Monthly muscle wasting assessment
  • Weight - Weekly

At-Home Treatment

  • SQ Fluids - Manage Stage 3/4 kidney disease months longer. Huge QoL advantage
  • Injectable Meds - Adequan, B12, Cerenia SQ at home. Saves money, reduces stress
  • Medication Timing - Optimize dosing schedules via pharmacokinetics knowledge
  • Blood Glucose - Home curves (diabetic dogs)
  • Wound Care - Wounds, pressure sores, bandaging

Case Management

  • Organize files - Timeline of diagnoses/treatments/responses
  • Graph trends - QoL, pain scores, SRR, weight. Trendlines > single data points
  • Flag interactions - Prevent drug interaction problems
  • Track research - VIN access = pull papers yourself

Weekly Monitoring Checklist

  • ☐ SRR recorded
  • ☐ Weight, BCS, MCS assessed
  • ☐ QoL scale + pain assessment completed
  • ☐ Trends graphed
  • ☐ Medications inventoried

Emotional Toll

Early grieving = knowing trajectory. Use as data for decisions, not for anticipatory grief torture.

Euthanasia Decision

Advantage: Understand process, recognize suffering, advocate for peaceful protocol.

Burden: Feel you "should know" exactly when. Guilt: "too soon?" or "too late?"

Permission Granted

Professional knowledge ≠ less entitled to emotion/grief/uncertainty. Assisting hundreds professionally ≠ facing your own dog's death. Be as compassionate with yourself as with clients.

Finding the Right Veterinary Partner

Need veterinarian comfortable with your knowledge level, willing to engage technically, respects you still need expertise (not client-speak).

Red Flags:

  • Dismissing concerns ("too close to see clearly")
  • Expecting everything at home ("you can")
  • Uncomfortable with questions/clinical language

Use knowledge for advocacy. But let others carry some burden. Don't be technician + diagnostician + case manager + grieving parent simultaneously.

Professional Resources

  • Canadian Veterinary Support: CVMA Wellness and provincial associations (OAVT, RVTTBC, etc.) - Professional support for veterinary technicians
  • VIN: vin.com - Research access and professional community
  • AAHA Guidelines: AAHA/IAAHPC End-of-Life Care Guidelines - Cite these when advocating
