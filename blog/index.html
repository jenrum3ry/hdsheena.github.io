Advocating for Your Senior Dog

A Journey Through Palliative Care

As our beloved companions age, they face unique health challenges that require special attention, understanding, and advocacy. This series is designed to help veterinary professionals and pet owners navigate the complex journey of senior dog care and palliative medicine.

Each article provides practical insights, evidence-based approaches, and compassionate guidance for ensuring your senior dog's comfort and quality of life during their golden years.

4. Multi-Modal Pain Management

Different medication classes, alternative therapies, and finding the right combination for your senior dog's comfort.

  • Why multi-modal matters: targeting different pain pathways
  • NSAIDs: carprofen, meloxicam, firocoxib, grapiprant
  • Gabapentin and pregabalin for neuropathic pain
  • Amantadine (NMDA receptor antagonist)
  • Adequan injections and Librela (monoclonal antibody)
  • Methadone for severe cases (diagnostic trial)
  • Alternative therapies: laser, acupuncture, physical rehabilitation
  • Evidence-based supplementation: omega-3s (why Glucosamine often fails), CBD oil
  • Additional medications: acetaminophen (vet-supervised only)
  • Combination protocol examples for different severity levels

6. Feeding the Senior Dog

Addressing digestive sensitivities, weight management, nutritional supplements, and special dietary considerations for aging dogs.

  • Changing nutritional needs: metabolism, protein, digestion
  • Why low-protein 'Senior' diets cause muscle wasting (unless Stage 3+ Kidney Disease)
  • Weight management: Body Condition Score, portion control
  • Digestive issues: sensitivities, probiotics, fiber, hydration
  • Disease-specific diets: kidney, heart, diabetes, arthritis
  • Supplements: omega-3s, glucosamine, MCT oil, SAMe
  • Practical challenges: decreased appetite, weight loss, difficulty eating
  • High-calorie recovery diets for unintended weight loss
  • Hydration strategies: multiple water stations, fountains

7. The Ketamine Conversation

Understanding ketamine therapy for chronic pain, informed discussions with veterinarians, and what to expect from this treatment option.

  • What ketamine is: anesthetic at high doses, analgesic at low doses
  • How it works: NMDA receptor antagonism, "turning down" pain signals
  • Administration options: IV infusion (CRI), oral (poor bioavailability), subcutaneous injections
  • SQ protocol: 0.5mg/kg with Loading Phase (frequent dosing for first week), then biweekly to weekly maintenance
  • Ideal candidates: chronic pain not controlled by conventional medications
  • Safety, side effects, and contraindications
  • Cost considerations and realistic expectations
  • How to have the conversation with your veterinarian
  • When ketamine isn't enough: methadone trials, specialist referral

9. The RVT Advantage

Using your professional knowledge to advocate effectively during appointments and bridge the gap between clinical care and home management.

  • Leveraging clinical skills: monitoring trends (SRR, BCS, MCS), speaking 'Vet' to get better care
  • At-home interventions: SQ fluids, injectable medications (Adequan, B12, Cerenia)
  • Tracking trendlines: graphing QoL scores, pain scores, weight for objective data
  • Peer-to-peer conversations: using technical language (central sensitization, NMDA antagonists)
  • When to speak up: citing AAHA guidelines, presenting clinical data
  • Professional network: VIN access, specialist referrals, research literature
  • Managing the emotional toll: anticipatory grief, compassion fatigue
  • Finding veterinary partners who respect your knowledge
