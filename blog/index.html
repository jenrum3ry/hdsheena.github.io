Advocating for Your Senior Dog

A Journey Through Palliative Care

As our beloved companions age, they face unique health challenges that require special attention, understanding, and advocacy. This series is designed to help veterinary professionals and pet owners navigate the complex journey of senior dog care and palliative medicine.

Each article provides practical insights, evidence-based approaches, and compassionate guidance for ensuring your senior dog's comfort and quality of life during their golden years.

4. Multi-Modal Pain Management

  • Why multi-modal matters: targeting different pain pathways

  • Opioids: tramadol, methadone for severe cases
  • Amantadine (NMDA receptor antagonist)
  • Adequan injections and Librela (monoclonal antibody)
  • Alternative therapies: laser, acupuncture, physical rehabilitation
  • Supplements: omega-3s, glucosamine, CBD oil
  • Additional medications: acetaminophen (vet-supervised only)

7. The Ketamine Conversation

Understanding ketamine therapy for chronic pain, informed discussions with veterinarians, and what to expect from this treatment option.

  • What ketamine is: anesthetic at high doses, analgesic at low doses
  • How it works: NMDA receptor antagonism, "turning down" pain signals
  • Administration options: IV infusion (CRI), oral, subcutaneous injections
  • SQ protocol example: 0.5mg/kg, biweekly to weekly dosing
  • Ideal candidates: chronic pain not controlled by conventional medications
  • Safety, side effects, and contraindications
  • Cost considerations and realistic expectations
  • How to have the conversation with your veterinarian
  • When ketamine isn't enough: methadone trials, specialist referral
